### Customer Churn Analysis (Telecom)

## Project Overview
Customer churn is a major challenge for telecom companies as losing customers directly impacts revenue.  
This project analyzes telecom customer data to identify patterns and factors that contribute to customer churn.

The goal is to use data analysis and visualization to understand customer behavior and help businesses make data-driven decisions to improve customer retention.

---

## Business Problem
Telecom companies experience customer loss when users cancel their services.  
Understanding why customers leave helps businesses improve services and reduce churn.

This project answers questions like:
- What is the overall churn rate?
- Which customer groups are more likely to churn?
- Does contract type influence churn?
- Are higher monthly charges related to churn?
- Does customer tenure affect retention?

---
## Dataset
Dataset used: Telco Customer Churn Dataset
Number of records: 7043 customers

## Data Analysis Process
1. Data Cleaning
- Converted Total Charges column to numeric format
- Handled missing values
- Checked for duplicates
- Verified data types

2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
   Analyzed customer behavior using visualizations such as:

- Customer churn distribution
- Contract type vs churn
- Tenure vs churn
- Monthly charges vs churn
- Internet service vs churn

3. Dashboard Creation
   Built an interactive Power BI dashboard to visualize churn patterns and help stakeholders                      	explore the data.

---

## Dashboard KPIs

- Total Customers
- Churn Customers
- Churn Rate
- Average Monthly Charges

---

## Key Insights

1. Customers with month-to-month contracts have the highest churn rate compared to long-term contracts.

2. Customers with short tenure (0–12 months) are more likely to churn than long-term customers.

3. Customers paying higher monthly charges show a higher tendency to churn.

4. Fiber optic internet users have higher churn rates compared to DSL users.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Encourage customers to switch to long-term contracts by offering discounts.
- Improve customer onboarding experience during the first few months.
- Provide better service bundles or pricing options for high monthly charges.
- Improve service quality and customer support for fiber internet users.
